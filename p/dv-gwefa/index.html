§ 04

If Something Trips

Note I

A breaker has tripped

Open the box, locate the lever in the lower position, switch off everything connected to that circuit, and lift the lever back up. If it trips again immediately, leave it off and call us — there is a fault on the circuit.

Note II

The voltage relay has cut power

This is protection working as intended. The grid voltage went outside the safe window. The relay will re-close on its own once voltage stabilises, after the programmed delay.

Note III

Switching to the generator

Start the generator first, let it stabilise, then move the UPS feed to the generator inlet. Reverse the order when mains return: bring the UPS back onto mains before shutting the generator down so the load never drops.

Note IV

Monthly RCD test

Once a month, press the small T button on each RCBO. The lever should snap down. Lift it back up. This confirms the residual-current protection is alive.

Note V

UPS alarm or beep

A short beep on outage is normal — the UPS is on battery. A long continuous beep means the battery is low or a fault has occurred; shed non-essential outlets and call us.

Note VI

Total blackout

If nothing in the box has tripped and the whole site is dark, it is a building-side outage, not your panel. Wait for supply to return — or run on generator if needed. The relay handles re-energisation safely.
